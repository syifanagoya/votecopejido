Early Life & Biography

Ninja Twitch was born in the Detroit area to American parents of Welsh descent. When he was 1, his family shifted to the Chicago suburbs, where he spent his youth. He used to spend his major time in playing sports and video games at that time.

He completed his schooling from Grayslake Central High School. During his high school years, he got a lot of opportunities to play soccer. He became an avid video game player. After getting graduated, he planned to take video games as his professional career. He not just entered into tournaments joined professional organizations, and also performed live streaming in his games.

Personal Life

From being a ‘leisure’ video game player to becoming a professional video gamer, Ninja turned his hobby into his profession due to his deep admiration for video games.

At the time when he was at the top of his career and was rejoicing in the success, he came across a sudden and a major downfall.  It has occurred in the way of health issues. He seemed to have lost his vision. He had undergone a surgery for the correction of his retinal defects. It was a very terrible phase in his life. For several months, he was completely bedridden.

He saw a drastic decline in his career. Where earlier he used to have around 4500 viewers on twitch, it had fallen to only 400 viewers. But his inner drive to excel contributed to making a comeback with a lot of determination and will to succeed. He put all critical remarks aside and focussed on grabbing every single opportunity to play big tournaments and become the ‘hero of e-sports.’

In terms of his love life, Ninja got married to his fellow gamer named Jessica Goch, in the year 2017. They both have known each other since his initial years in video gaming.

Ninja is a humanitarian and has been involved in several charity works. In February 2018, he participated in a fundraising charity event, where he donated around $110,000 to the US Foundation for Suicide Prevention. He donated nearly $50,000 of the prize money that he received from the success of Fortnite Battle in April 2018 to the Alzheimer’s Association. In 2018, he participated in a Kids event to raise more than $340,000 for the welfare of children.

There is one interesting thing to note about Ninja. While he is very much enthusiastic about playing video games, he does not encourage children to play it. He has never promoted professional video gaming as the right career option for every child!

By the end of the year 2017, he garnered more than 500,000 followers. By 2018, this number rose by 250 percent.
