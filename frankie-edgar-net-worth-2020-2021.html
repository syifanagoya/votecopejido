Early Life

Frankie Edgar was born in the year 1981 on 16th October in New Jersey in America. He attended the Toms River High School East. He has two other siblings and is the oldest among them. Even as a child, he started wrestling at the public school. He was so good at it that he soon made it to the New Jersey state championship tournament and was placed 2nd in it.

Even as a senior in high school, he came 2nd in the NHSCA Senior Nationals. He took admission in the Clarion University of Pennsylvania. Frankie continued to wrestle for four years while being in the university, and he qualified for the nationals.

Career

Frankie Edgar was keen on starting a career as a wrestler and trained with Ricardo Almeida. He has trained and fought with many other wrestlers like Rob Guarino and boxed with Mark Henry. Other than martial arts, he has mastered other forms of wrestling like the Brazilian Jiu-jitsu, which is a combination of martial arts and combat sports systems, which mainly focuses on ground fighting.

To kick-start his career, he moved to Las Vegas to be a part of the American mixed martial arts organization named the Ultimate Fighting Championship. However, he was not selected at first but was later called on by their president. He was asked to fight against the undefeated wrestler named Tyson Griffin in a martial arts event organized by them named UFC 67: All or Nothing. Edgar won the fight. He also won in other martial arts events like the UFC 73: Stacked and UFC 78: Validation. His first loss was in the UFC Fight Night 13. He fought Gary Maynard in the event.

He won the UFC Lightweight Championship in the year 2010. He won the Fight of the Night honours from his fight against Benson Henderson in the year 2012 at the UFC 144. In the same year that is 2012, Frankie moved to featherweight division from lightweight division. He also fought in the reality TV show, The Ultimate Fighter.

Apart from being an excellent wrestler in the UFC, Edgar became a part of the KHK MMA Fight Team. In the year 2016, he signed with the greatest martial arts promotion in Bahrain, Brave Combat Federation and became the colour commentator or the expert commentator. His first commenting was the Brave 3: Battle in Brazil in the year 2017.

Awards and Achievements

Frankie Edgar has won various fights and championships. His hard work and his passion for martial arts have led him to success. He has won a total of 11 Ultimate Fighting Championship – one UFC Lightweight Championship, seven Fight of the Night award, two Performance of the Night award and one Knockout of the Night award. He has won Fight of the Year award in the year 2011. He has also won World MMA Awards, which was presented by Fighters Only magazine. Edgar also won a 2012 Fight of the Year award. He was nominated for Best Upset ESPY Award.
